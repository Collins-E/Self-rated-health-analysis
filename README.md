# Self-Rated Health Analysis (Canada)

## Overview
This project analyzes self-rated health trends across Canadian provinces from 2015 to 2024 using R. The goal is to understand how health perception has changed over time and identify differences across regions.

## Dataset
Source: Statistics Canada (Quality of Life Hub)

## Tools Used
- R
- dplyr
- tidyr
- ggplot2

## Key Analysis
- Data cleaning and transformation
- Trend analysis over time
- Provincial comparisons
- Change analysis (2015–2024)

## Summary
The analysis shows a generally stable trend in self-rated health from 2015 to 2019, followed by a noticeable decline after 2020. This drop aligns with major global events such as the COVID19 pandemic, which likely impacted both physical and mental health perceptions across Canada. While all provinces experienced declines, the magnitude varied.

## Key Insights
- Quebec consistently reported the highest self-rated health levels across most years
- Ontario remained slightly below the national average throughout the period
- Alberta experienced the largest overall decline from 2015 to 2024
- The national average decreased significantly after 2020, indicating a broad impact across regions
- Smaller provinces showed more variability, suggesting possible sensitivity to local factors
